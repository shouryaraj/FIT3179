Mount Everest (Nepali: Sagarmāthā; Chinese: Zhūmùlǎngmǎ; Tibetan: Chomolungma ) is Earth's highest mountain above sea level, located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas. Its elevation (snow height) of 8,848.86 m (29,031.7 ft) was most recently established in 2020 by the Nepali and Chinese authorities.
