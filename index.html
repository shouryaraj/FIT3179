Into Thin Air - 8848m

Mount Everest (Nepali: Sagarmāthā; Chinese: Zhūmùlǎngmǎ; Tibetan: Chomolungma ) is Earth's highest mountain above sea level, located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas. Its elevation (snow height) of 8,848.86 m (29,031.7 ft) was most recently established in 2020 by the Nepali and Chinese authorities.

Nepalese are the ones who have climbed Everest the most. The total count since 1953 to 2020 of successful summits for Nepalese is 4859.
It's not just they want to be the best. The major reason is that Everest offers bread and butter for them.

Sherpa is one of the Nepalese community and most of the summiteers are Sherpa.

Tenzing Norgay is one of the pioneers who climb Everest first. He also belongs to Sherpa Community.

They carry the heaviest loads and pay the highest prices on the world's tallest mountain. They make it possible for each Everest aspirant's ambitions to come true.

Junko Tabei

Japan

Junko Tabei, a Japanese climber, was the first woman to summit Everest in 1975 , leading an all-female crew. Since then, a growing number of female climbers have picked up the challenge. In comparison to Male Summiteers however, it is still a smaller number.
